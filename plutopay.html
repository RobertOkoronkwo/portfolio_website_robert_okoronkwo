PlutoPay

What is PlutoPay?

PlutoPay is a web responsive application which allows anyone to instantly transfer money digitally through the platform without the need of a physical bank or store.

The problem I resolved in this project was to better aid users in regards to their personal finances. Whether it is paying a friend for movie tickets, planning ahead for bill payments, or buying an item from a buy/sell/trade group where the need for an easy and intuitive digital payment system is available.

My Process

Research

I began by defining the problem for the case study then performing research based on similar applications such as PayPal, Zelle, and CashApp to develop a competitive edge.


Click here to view full research analysis

Ideation

Once a general foundation of PlutoPay was established, user interviews, stories, and task analysis helped towards later building the low fidelity wireframes. Creating user flows allowed me to develop process and decision points. Actions such as creating an account, sending money, and viewing reports were better understood.

Affinity Maps played a significant role by understanding the behaviors, attitudes, goals, needs, and frustrations. Information collected at this point helped in making critical functionality decisions.


Click here to view full ideation

Wireframes

I started the design process with low fidelity wireframes sketches. This is typically how I jot down ideas before moving towards mid fidelity wireframes. Once low fidelity sketches were developed, mid fidelity designs were created to build the first prototype. User testing let us discover what changes needed to be made based off participant feedback. The collected data from usability tests was essential towards the high fidelity prototype.

Design

In total, there were 27 screen created for this project. The overall aesthetic is aimed towards a relaxing and comfortable experience. Colors such as purple, lavender, sky blue, and white are heavily used to express this feeling.

Prototype

This prototype only showcases primary functions of the user experience and is not a fully developed application. Since we wanted to focus on three major functions: send money, view reports, and create a budget, the prototype was formed around completing these specific tasks.

Conclusion

As someone who likes to manage their finances and monitor their spending habits, I believe PlutoPay does an excellent job with providing the appropriate tools to do so. Most people tend to spend a lot of money on take out food or online shopping, disregarding how much their finances have been impacted. Budgeting on the app is a considerably useful feature for financial responsibility, as well as easy access to funds or online shopping. The minimalistic design approach makes the overall experience enjoyable while managing your finances.
